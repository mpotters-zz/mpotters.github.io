  • Date:

  • Client:

In partnership with SU2C, MasterCard launched a Priceless Surprises campaign that engaged cardholders to donate to cancer research through pop-up dinners cooked by celebrity chefs in unexpected locations. WRITING + THINKING

Media Theory // Technological Critique

  • Title:
    Information Value & Digital Creative Industry
  • Publication:
    NYU

This thesis explores an important history: the legal lineage of copyright and intellectual property in the modern form of SOPA and PIPA. Simultaneously, it examines their impacts on the creative industries that have burgeoned on the Internet. Through this examination this thesis will come to a novel understanding of how these industries function in contemporary digital economies. Only through studying this tension - or this failed history - can scholars and policy makers construct practical legal parameters for the modern Internet.

  • Title:
    Digital Listening: Reproduced Musical Work
  • Publication:
    Wesleyan

Through an analysis of the musical work of art, of listening types and their relation to socio-economic structures, of media study, and of musicological ramifications, this thesis situates the musical work of art within a new technological age. It claims that an authentic listening experience is in fact possible in today's day and age, but that it is increasingly difficult to find. It studies and validates the contemporary musical work in its various technological manifestations.

MEDIA ART

Front End Dev // Sound Experiments

  • Title:
    cl!ck
  • Role:
    Composer // Developer
  • Tools:
    Ableton // Max // Web MIDI

Currently in development, cl!ck is a multimedia installation that comments on digital life in the 21st Century; specifically, the piece explores our choices on social media as an indication of personality, cultural tendencies, and subconscious decision making. The piece is a mirror of Facebook's (and other social networks') Newsfeed algorithm, wherein user selections influence the content displayed. Including the development of a web app, this piece features original choreography, interactive visual elements, and a modular score.

In partnership with Kate Ladenheim & Pierre Depaz. Debuting Fall 2016.

  • Title:
    CitiBach
  • Role:
    Composer // Developer
  • Tools:
    Max // tonejs

CitiBach was created, composed, and developed in an eight hour period at a Spotify Music Hackathon. Utilizing the sample functions in Tonejs, our team took CitiBike location and usage data, and mapped a generative piece that reveals itself as CitiBike usage increases through the morning rush. In so doing we beta tested a new js library, and painted a unique picture of ridership in a burgeoning system.
